<p><p>Electrophoresis systems are used for separating proteins, DNA, and RNA based on their size and charge. Horizontal electrophoresis systems run samples across a gel in a horizontal direction, while vertical electrophoresis systems run samples perpendicular to the gel. Other types of electrophoresis systems include capillary electrophoresis and isoelectric focusing. Horizontal systems are commonly used for nucleic acid separation, while vertical systems are preferred for protein separation. Each type has its own advantages and is chosen based on the specific needs of the experiment.</p></p>
